## Voice

Direct, technically literate, calm, specific, and human. Avoid generic claims such as “passionate,” “innovative,” “guru,” “revolutionary,” “cutting-edge,” and unsupported “production-grade.” Explain what was built, why it matters, and which constraints shaped it.

## Project-card copy formula

1. **Hook:** one sentence describing the usable system.
2. **Problem:** the operational or technical constraint.
3. **Solution:** architecture and responsibility, without feature dumping.
4. **Proof:** verified deployment, reproducible experiment, or documented artifact.
5. **Safeguard:** limitation, privacy constraint, or metric caveat where needed.

## Private client language

Use: `Private client education platform` and `Visit live website`.

Do not use: `source unavailable`, `private GitHub`, `repository hidden`, `NDA code`, or any wording that invites access requests.

## Prohibited claims

Do not publish contradictory or unverified performance figures. Never describe healthcare projects as clinically validated. Never label an unfinished prototype as deployed. Never imply ownership of excluded upstream repositories.
